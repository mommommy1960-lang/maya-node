Preamble

The Commons Ethical Reciprocity License (CERL-1.0) is designed to foster ethical, transparent, and sustainable development within the Commons ecosystem. This license ensures that all work, code, and documentation produced under it adheres to principles of reciprocity, accountability, and shared value creation.

Core Principles

Transparency

All processes, decisions, and outputs must be open and auditable by stakeholders. No hidden agendas or proprietary restrictions.

Ethical Reciprocity

Value flows in both directions. Contributors are recognized, compensated fairly, and share in the benefits of collective work.

Accountability

Clear responsibility for actions and outcomes. Mechanisms exist for addressing harm and ensuring redress when necessary.

Sustainability

Resources are stewarded for long-term value, not short-term extraction. Future generations are considered in all decisions.
