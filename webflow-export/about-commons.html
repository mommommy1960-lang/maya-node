About the Commons

Understanding the governance framework and ethical principles that guide our work

What is the Commons?

Definition

The Commons represents a new paradigm for technology development—one that prioritizes shared value, ethical governance, and transparent collaboration over extraction and monopolization.

It's an ecosystem where knowledge, tools, and frameworks are developed openly, governed transparently, and shared equitably among all contributors and stakeholders.

Core Values

  • Transparency in all processes and decisions
  • Ethical reciprocity in value exchange
  • Sustainable stewardship of shared resources
  • Inclusive participation and governance
  • Accountability to all stakeholders

CERL-1.0: Commons Ethical Reciprocity License

The governance framework that ensures ethical, transparent, and sustainable development

Transparency

All processes, decisions, and code are open and auditable. No hidden agendas, no proprietary black boxes—just clear, documented engineering.

Reciprocity

Value flows both ways. Contributors are recognized, compensated, and credited. Benefits are shared equitably among all participants.

Sustainability

Long-term thinking over short-term extraction. Resources are stewarded responsibly for future generations.

Accountability

Clear responsibility for all actions and outcomes. Mechanisms for redress when harm occurs. No one is above the governance framework.

Ethics

Moral principles guide all decisions. Harm prevention is prioritized. The wellbeing of people and communities comes first.

Inclusivity

Diverse voices are welcomed and amplified. Decision-making processes are accessible to all stakeholders, not just technical experts.

Why the Commons Matters

Traditional tech development has created massive inequality, environmental harm, and extractive power structures. The surveillance economy, data exploitation, and algorithmic bias are not accidents—they're features of systems designed without ethical constraints.

The Commons offers an alternative: technology that serves human flourishing, respects autonomy, and creates shared prosperity. It's not utopian—it's practical, proven, and already working in communities around the world.

MayaNode brings Commons principles to engineering documentation and technical frameworks, proving that ethical development is not just possible—it's superior.
