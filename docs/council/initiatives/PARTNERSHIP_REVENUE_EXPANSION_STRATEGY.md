## Executive Summary

This document outlines the strategic approach to building revenue-generating partnerships that accelerate Maya Node's growth while maintaining alignment with Commons principles. Our partnership strategy focuses on creating win-win relationships that expand market reach, enhance product capabilities, and generate sustainable revenue streams.

**Key Goals:**
- Establish 5+ strategic partnerships generating $200K+ combined value in 2025
- Create 3+ co-marketing programs reaching 50K+ potential customers
- Build ecosystem of 10+ integration partners
- Secure $50K+ in cloud credits and infrastructure support
- Generate 30%+ of new customers through partnership channels

## I. Partnership Framework

### 1.1 Partnership Principles

All partnerships must align with:

**CERL-1.0 Compliance:**
- Partners must acknowledge and respect CERL-1.0 constraints
- No partnerships with surveillance, weapons, or harmful technology companies
- All joint offerings must maintain ethical standards
- Right to terminate partnership if CERL-1.0 is violated

**Mutual Value Creation:**
- Win-win outcomes for both parties
- Clear value proposition for each partner
- Measurable success metrics
- Fair revenue or benefit sharing

**Mission Alignment:**
- Partners should share Commons values (or be open to them)
- Support for ethical AI, transparency, and user sovereignty
- Commitment to social impact and responsibility

**Long-term Thinking:**
- Partnerships designed for multi-year collaboration
- Investment in joint success
- Flexibility to evolve as both organizations grow

### 1.2 Partnership Types

**1. Technology Partners**
- Cloud infrastructure providers (AWS, GCP, Azure)
- AI/ML platform companies (Hugging Face, Anthropic)
- Developer tool vendors (GitHub, GitLab)
- Security and compliance platforms

**2. Go-to-Market Partners**
- System integrators and consulting firms
- Resellers and value-added resellers (VARs)
- Industry-specific solution providers
- Marketing and lead generation partners

**3. Ecosystem Partners**
- Open source projects and foundations
- Standards bodies and industry groups
- Academic and research institutions
- Community and advocacy organizations

**4. Strategic Partners**
- Complementary product companies
- Accessibility technology leaders
- Enterprise software vendors
- Government and civic tech organizations

## IV. Revenue Models & Commercial Terms

### 4.1 Partnership Revenue Models

**1. Referral Fees**
- 10-15% of first-year contract value
- Lower overhead, passive income
- Good for initial partnerships

**2. Revenue Share**
- 5-10% of ongoing revenue from referred customers
- Aligns incentives long-term
- Better for strategic partners

**3. Licensing Fees**
- Flat annual fee for integration or white-label use
- $50K-$150K depending on partner size and use case
- Predictable revenue

**4. Co-Sell / Joint Go-to-Market**
- Shared sales and marketing costs
- Both parties sell actively
- Revenue split based on contribution

**5. Integration Marketplace**
- Maya Node listed on partner marketplace (AWS, Salesforce, etc.)
- Standard marketplace terms (typically 20-30% to marketplace)
- High volume potential

### 4.2 Partner Program Tiers

**Bronze (Free)**
- Listed in partner directory
- Access to documentation and APIs
- Community support
- Self-service integration

**Silver ($2K-$5K/year)**
- Co-marketing opportunities (logo, case study)
- Technical support
- Training for 2-3 people
- Quarterly partner calls

**Gold ($10K-$20K/year)**
- Dedicated partner manager
- Custom integrations supported
- Joint go-to-market planning
- Unlimited training
- Revenue share or referral fees

**Platinum (Custom)**
- Strategic partnership agreement
- Joint product development
- Executive sponsorship
- Custom commercial terms

### 4.3 Sample Commercial Terms

**Example: Cloud Partner (AWS)**
- No cash fees
- $50K credits to Maya Node
- Listing on AWS Marketplace (AWS takes 5-15%)
- Joint marketing (2-3 activities per year)
- Quarterly business reviews

**Example: SI Partner (Consulting Firm)**
- No upfront fee
- 15% referral fee on first-year contract value
- Discounted training for partner consultants (50% off)
- Joint case studies and co-marketing
- Annual partnership review

**Example: Technology Integration (Salesforce)**
- $75K annual integration license

- Joint go-to-market support
- Technical integration support from both sides
- Quarterly product roadmap alignment

## V. Partnership Operations

### 5.1 Partnership Lifecycle

**1. Identification & Targeting (2-4 weeks)**
- Research potential partners
- Assess strategic fit
- Initial outreach via warm intro if possible

**2. Exploration & Qualification (4-8 weeks)**
- Discovery calls
- Mutual value assessment
- Technical feasibility review
- Leadership alignment

**3. Proposal & Negotiation (4-12 weeks)**
- Partnership proposal document
- Commercial terms discussion
- Legal review
- Contract negotiation

**4. Onboarding & Enablement (4-8 weeks)**
- Partnership agreement signed
- Teams introduced
- Training and enablement
- Integration setup (if applicable)
- Go-to-market plan finalized

**5. Execution & Optimization (Ongoing)**
- Regular partnership calls (monthly or quarterly)
- Pipeline and deal review
- Co-marketing activities
- Performance metrics tracking
- Continuous improvement

**6. Renewal & Expansion (Annual)**
- Partnership performance review
- Renewal negotiation
- Expansion opportunities
- Adjustment of terms if needed

### 5.2 Partnership Metrics & KPIs

**For Each Partnership, Track:**

- **Pipeline Generated:** Number and value of opportunities from partner
- **Closed Deals:** Revenue attributed to partnership
- **Co-Marketing Activities:** Webinars, case studies, events, etc.
- **Customer Satisfaction:** NPS or satisfaction scores for partner-sourced customers
- **Partner Engagement:** Frequency of interactions, responsiveness
- **Time to Value:** How quickly partnership generates results
- **ROI:** Return on investment (revenue vs. cost of partnership)

**Quarterly Partnership Scorecard:**
- Traffic/leads generated by partner
- Sales pipeline from partner
- Closed revenue from partner
- Co-marketing activities completed
- Partner satisfaction score
- Overall partnership health (Red/Yellow/Green)

## VIII. Risk Mitigation

### 8.1 Partnership Risks

**Risk: Partner doesn't deliver expected value**
- **Mitigation:** Clear success metrics upfront, regular reviews, exit clauses
- **Contingency:** Shift resources to other partners

**Risk: Partner violates CERL-1.0 principles**
- **Mitigation:** Clear ethical guidelines in contract, monitoring, termination rights
- **Contingency:** Immediate termination, public communication about values

**Risk: Over-dependence on one partner**
- **Mitigation:** Diversify partnership portfolio, no single partner >30% of value
- **Contingency:** Build direct channels to balance partnership channels

**Risk: Integration technical challenges**
- **Mitigation:** Proof of concept before commitment, phased rollout, dedicated engineering
- **Contingency:** Pause integration, seek alternative partners

**Risk: Partnership opportunity cost**
- **Mitigation:** Selective focus on highest-impact partnerships, say no often
- **Contingency:** Quarterly review and pruning of underperforming partnerships
